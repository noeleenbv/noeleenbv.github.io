Daylesford

Microsite shop design & development

A responsive microsite shop created for the brand — Daylesford Organic Farm Gloucestershire.

I was involved with the project from kick–off / concept stage through to completion, responsible for the design, coding and testing. Working alongside a project manager who liaised directly with the client for acquiring assets, feedback and approval. The shop was designed in Fireworks, coded in Dreamweaver and then uploaded on to the Ocado website using an in–house content management system. Pages were tested on hidden aisle links, signed-off internally and then sent to the client for approval.

The shop features HD video content in drop down banners on various pages, such as on the "Home" and "Our Dairy" pages.

Pampers

Email design & coding

A baby incentive email that was designed to encourage customers to shop. Customers received five free packs of Pampers nappies with their order when they applied the voucher code at checkout.

The email was based on a standard template and followed the look and feel of a baby event that was live on the Ocado website at the time. I was responsible for the design, coding and testing.

Designed in Fireworks, coded in Dreamweaver and tested using Litmus and an in–house platform.

  • Date: 2015
  • Client: Ocado / Pampers

Ocado

Christmas microsite shop design

A responsive microsite shop that was created for the Ocado Christmas event in 2013.

For this massive project, which consisted of about sixty pages, I worked together with colleagues in art direction, design, copy, photography and development. My responsibilities involved cutting out product shots, page design / layout and final checks once live.

Talifun

Website design & development

Website design and build for Talifun Ltd. This site was done as part of a final exam project for an Adobe Dreamweaver and Flash course.

I created all the assets including the logo, icon sets and flash animations. Hand coded the HTML / CSS and implemented basic jQuery into the site to hook up bookmarks & social links, Twitter feeds, Disqus and Beauty of code. Master template pages were first created in Dreamweaver and then moved over into Umbraco (CMS).

Live link: http://www.talifun.com

  • Date: 2011
  • Client: Talifun
